Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Securing your university accounts with an extra layer of protection.

Your Guide to Secure Multi-Factor Authentication

1. What is MFA?

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is a security measure that requires users to verify their identity using two or more factors, rather than relying solely on a password. This adds an extra layer of protection, making it harder for unauthorized users to access accounts or systems even if a password is compromised.

All students and staff are required to set up MFA.

2. Setting Up MFA

Follow the user guides attached below according to your role to set up your Multi-Factor Authentication:

SIBT Students: SIBT MFA Setup Guide WSUSCC Students: WSU MFA Guide SIBT Teachers/Staff: Navitas MFA Guide

At any point, if you require assistance in setting up MFA, please contact iHelp Tech Support.

3. Important MFA Notes & Troubleshooting

Secondary Access: Please add your contact number during MFA setup. This will work as a secondary access method to get a code to your phone via SMS if your authenticator app ever crashes or is inaccessible.

Changing Mobile Devices: If you have changed your mobile device, please set up MFA on your new device while having your old device at hand. If you require assistance, please contact the iHelp team to assist you.

Western Students/Staff: If you do not have your old device and need to set up MFA on a new device, please contact Main Campus IT directly to get MFA set up on your new device.

**General Troubleshooting:** Encountering other issues with your MFA? Here are some common problems and solutions:

  • **Authenticator App Not Working:** Ensure your phone's time is set to automatic. Re-sync the app if necessary.
  • **Not Receiving Codes:** Check your phone's signal or verify your registered phone number.
  • **Lost/Stolen Device:** Immediately contact the IT Service Desk to reset your MFA and regain access.
